General  Instructions  for All Teams  
One Theme,  Two  Deliverables:   
• Team  Formation:  Students  must  form  a team  consisting  of a minimum  of 3 and a 
maximum  of 5 members . 
• Theme  Selection:  Each  team  must  select  exactly  one theme  from  the 10 options  
provided  below.  
• Project  + Assignment  Link:  The chosen  theme  will serve  as the foundation  for both 
your Mini  Project  (design,  development,  and deployment)  and your Assignment  
(technical  documentation).  
• Depth  of Work:  By focusing  on a single  theme,  the team  will deeply  explore  the 
mathematical  formulations , implement  the optimization  algorithm , analyze  results,  
and formally  document  the findings  in the final report.  
Part  A: Project  (20 Marks)  
 
Objective:  Design, Develop, and Deploy  
For your chosen theme, your team is expected to build a fully functional machine learning 
pipeline that implements the specific numerical optimization technique outlined in the theme's 
abstract.  
Project  Requirements:  
• Design:  Formulate  the mathematical  objective  function  and the specific  optimization  
strategy  (e.g.,  adaptive  gradient  methods,  proximal  methods,  Augmented  Lagrangians)  
required  by your theme.  
• Develop:  Implement  the custom  optimizer  and train your model  on the dataset  
explicitly  specified  in your theme's  abstract  (e.g.,  CIFAR -10, TalkingData,  Chest  X-
Ray).  Compare  the performance  of your custom  implementation  against  standard  
baselines.

• Deploy/Execute:  Ensure  your code  is modular,  reproducible,  and executable.  You 
must  evaluate  the performance  of your algorithm  and assess  its suitability  for the 
specific  machine  learning  problem.  
 
Part  B: Assignment  (10 Marks)  
 
Objective:  Draft a technical documentation  (6-12 Pages)  
Based strictly  on the work you completed in Part A , draft a comprehensive technical 
documentation  detailing your methodology, mathematical formulations, and experimental 
results.  
Assignment  Requirements:  
• Format:  The submission must be a properly formatted 6–12-page  document.  
• Content:  Your  article  must  translate  your practical  implementation  into a formal  
report.  It should  clearly  discuss  the strengths  and limitations  of the optimization  
technique  you applied.  
• Analysis:  Include  empirical  evidence  (graphs,  tables,  convergence  rates)  proving  how 
your applied  optimization  technique  improved  the performance  of the machine  learning  
model.  Discuss  the specific  challenges  addressed  in your theme,  such as vanishing  
gradients,  high-dimensional  feature  selection,  or communication  bottlenecks  in 
distributed  networks.  
• Plagiarism  Policy:  A plagiarism  report  must  be submitted  along  with the final 
document.  The similarity  index  must  be less than 15%,  excluding  references,  equations,  
and standard  technical  terminology.  Submissions  exceeding  this threshold  will not be 
evaluated.

Theme -8: Conjugate  Gradient -Backed  Hessian -Free  Optimization  for Deep  Vision
